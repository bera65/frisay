# Frisay Module Ecosystem License & Developer Policy

## Overview

Frisay is an open-source e-commerce platform designed to encourage community-driven development. Developers are welcome to create, distribute, share, and sell modules, themes, integrations, and extensions for the Frisay ecosystem.

Our goal is to build an open marketplace where innovation is encouraged while maintaining compatibility and quality standards.

---

# Developer Rights

As a Frisay developer, you are free to:

* Create modules, themes, and integrations for Frisay.
* Distribute your work free of charge.
* Sell commercial modules and themes.
* Offer support, customization, and consulting services related to your products.
* Publish your projects on GitHub, your own website, or third-party marketplaces.
* Use Frisay's public APIs and extension system.

Ownership of your original code remains with you unless you explicitly release it under an open-source license.

---

# Commercial Modules

Developers may create and sell commercial products for Frisay without paying royalties to the Frisay project.

Examples include:

* Payment gateway integrations
* Shipping integrations
* Marketing tools
* ERP integrations
* Marketplace connectors
* Premium themes
* Analytics tools
* AI-powered extensions

Pricing, licensing, and support terms are determined entirely by the developer.

---

# Open Source Contributions

Contributions to the Frisay core project are welcomed.

By submitting code to the Frisay core repository, contributors agree that:

* Their contributions may be modified, improved, or maintained by the Frisay project.
* The project maintainers may include the contribution in future releases.
* Appropriate attribution will be preserved whenever practical.

---

# Compatibility Requirements

Modules and themes should:

* Use documented APIs and extension points whenever possible.
* Avoid modifying Frisay core files directly.
* Follow the official module development guidelines.
* Remain compatible with future Frisay releases whenever possible.
* Respect user privacy and applicable laws.

Products that intentionally damage, bypass, or compromise Frisay installations are prohibited.

---

# Branding

Developers may state that their products are:

* "Compatible with Frisay"
* "Built for Frisay"
* "Designed for Frisay"

However, developers may not:

* Claim official endorsement when none exists.
* Use Frisay trademarks in a misleading manner.
* Present third-party products as official Frisay products.

We encourage developers to:

* Share knowledge openly.
* Contribute improvements back to the ecosystem.

Frisay is built around openness, extensibility, and developer freedom.

Anyone may develop, distribute, or sell modules and themes for Frisay while retaining ownership of their work and respecting the project's development standards.
